Since 2006

The Lake Group Story

From a single fuel station to a pan-African conglomerate, the journey of Lake Group since 2006.

Timeline

Two Decades of Growth

A story of bold decisions, relentless expansion, and building an energy empire from the ground up.

2006

Lake Oil Founded

Ally Edha Awadh founds Lake Oil Ltd. at age 27 in Dar es Salaam, Tanzania, the seed of what would become Lake Group. Begins petroleum distribution with a small fleet.

2008

Logistics & First Regional Steps

Lake Trans Ltd. is established as the Group's logistics arm. The same year, DRC Petroleum S.A.R.L. is founded in the Democratic Republic of Congo and Lake Petroleum Ltd. is incorporated in Zambia the Group's first ventures beyond Tanzania.

2010

GCCP Ready-Mix Concrete

Gulf Concrete and Cement Products Company (GCCP) is established, quickly becoming the market leader in ready-mix concrete supply in Dar es Salaam.

2012

East African Footprint Grows

Burundi Petroleum opens its first petrol station in Burundi, and Lake Petroleum Rwanda established in Kigali in 2011 begins importing and distributing fuel across Rwanda.

2014

Lubricants & Composite Cylinders

Lake Lubes Ltd. is established in Dar es Salaam to blend and market the Group's own lubricants, and Lake Gas launches Africa's first composite LPG cylinders non-explosive, lightweight and translucent.

2017

Kenya Entry at Scale

The Competition Authority of Kenya approves Lake Oil's acquisition of Hashi Energy's Kenyan fuel-station network a landmark step into East Africa's largest retail fuel market.

2018

Tanga LPG Terminal

Lake Gas invests in Tanga, commissioning what opens as East Africa's largest LPG storage terminal 1,000 MT of capacity, receiving ships through a dedicated single point mooring buoy.

2019

Lake Steel Begins Production

Lake Steel and Allied Products starts operations at Visiga, Kibaha the first producer of high-strength, corrosion-resistant (HS-CR) rebar in Tanzania, with an annual capacity of 100,000 metric tonnes.

2022

Recognition & LPG Expansion

The Tanga LPG terminal is expanded to 3,000 MT, and founder Ally Edha Awadh is named Young Business Leader of the Year by the African Leadership Magazine.

Today

4,600+ Employees. 8 Countries. Still Growing.

Lake Group continues to expand, deepening its regional footprint, investing in new technologies, and driving economic development across East and Central Africa.
