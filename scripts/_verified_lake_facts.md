## Divisions / subsidiaries

### Lake Oil Ltd (Tanzania — flagship, 2006)
- Leading oil marketing company in Tanzania; one of top-5 petroleum distributors. [official]
- Kigamboni (Dar es Salaam) depot: 38 million litres storage capacity, direct pipeline
  access to oil import jetty. [official app_config TA.OIL; East Africa Today]
- 152 fuel stations (group source of truth, 2026). [official] site stats
- Own logistics fleet of 300 tankers (Lake Oil's own fleet, distinct from Lake Trans). [official app_config TA.OIL]
- Stations offer convenience stores, car washes, co-branded fast food, mechanical
  services, LUKU prepaid electricity, ATMs; open 24 hours. [official app_config]
- Bunkering services for vessels calling at Dar es Salaam port. [official app_config TA.OIL]
- Client segments: railways, mining, fish processing, port operators, truckers, public
  transport, government stores, relief agencies, road contractors, hotels, marine vessels,
  retail stations. [official app_config]
- Address: Plot 72 & 73, Vijibweni Area, Kigamboni, P.O. Box 5055, Dar es Salaam. [official app_config]
- 2017: Competition Authority of Kenya approved Lake Oil's acquisition of Hashi Energy's
  Kenyan fuel-station network. [press Forbes 2017]
- Mbeya depot also exists. [press Forbes 2017]

### Lake Trans Ltd (2008 — second company of the group)
- Logistics arm; transports petroleum products locally and in transit. [official]
- Fleet (official figures conflict): 750 vehicles incl. 200 local tankers + 500 transit
  tankers + 150 flatbeds (text); "more than 850 vehicles" (title); "owns and operates
  about 500 trucks" (same page). Tanker capacities 12,000–40,000 litres. [official app_config TA.TRANS]
- Every truck GPS-tracked. [official]
- Workshops: Kibaha (main), Kigamboni, Morogoro; Nairobi (Kenya); Ndola (Zambia). [official]
- Member of Tanzania Truck Owners Association (TATOA). [official]
- Transports to Zambia, Rwanda, DRC, Burundi, Malawi, Kenya, Uganda. [official]
- Contractor for Trafigura and Augusta (Agusta Energy). [official; press Forbes]

### Lake Gas Ltd
- Retail and bulk LPG in Tanzania, Zambia, DRC, Kenya, Burundi, Rwanda. [official]
- Depot & filling facilities: Dar es Salaam (Kigamboni), Mwanza, Kahama, Arusha, Iringa,
  Zanzibar. [official app_config]
- Kigamboni terminal: 750 MT (≈1,600,000 litres) LPG storage; receives product from ships
  via 3,500 m pipeline from Dar port oil jetty; filling plant >40 MT/day; cylinder
  warehouse >20,000 cylinders; 15 LPG road tankers; 3 bobtails (two 6 MT, one 12 MT);
  small bulk tanks 2–5 MT for large consumers. [official app_config TA.GAS]
- Tanga terminal: built as East Africa's largest LPG storage facility, 1,000 MT (two
  500 MT bullets) with private single mooring buoy; official page notes further bullets
  under construction. Later expanded to 3,000 MT (opened 2022; started 2018 at 1,000 MT
  per Absa Bank MD). [official app_config; press Africa Energy Insights]
- First to introduce composite LPG cylinders in Africa/Tanzania; launched 18 June 2014.
  [official app_config NEWS; press]
- Cylinder range: 6 kg (domestic), 15 kg and 38 kg (hotels/restaurants), 10 kg composite.
  [official lakegas page]
- 200+ dealers nationwide; "Q&Q — Quality with Quantity" tamper-proof seal. [official]

### Lake Lubes Ltd (2014)
- Lubricant & grease manufacturing, distribution, marketing; incorporated in Dar es
  Salaam. Slogan "Committed to Quality". [official app_config]
- Sold via Lake Oil station network in Tanzania + group presence in Zambia, Burundi,
  Rwanda, Kenya, DRC, UAE. [official]
- Product lines include LAKE 4T, LAKE HD SUPREME 700/500, LAKE HD, LAKE POWER (+ SUPREME,
  ECO), LAKE GL-5 gear oils, LAKE ATF D II, LAKE SUPER COOL coolant, LAKE HYDROTECH AW 68,
  LAKE GEAR EP 320, LAKE GREASE MP 2/MP 3/EP 2. [official app_config]
- After-sales: 24/7 lubrication consultation, technical training, site surveys,
  troubleshooting, used-oil analysis interpretation. [official]

### Lake Steel and Allied Products Ltd
- First company in Tanzania to introduce high-strength corrosion-resistant (HS-CR)
  reinforcement steel bar. [official lakesteel page]
- Fully computerized automatic rolling mill, up to 25 tonnes/hour, ~100,000 MT/year. [official]
- HS-CR bar retains strength at 600°C (vs. ordinary rebar losing strength above 350°C);
  ≥50% higher corrosion resistance than ordinary rebar. [official]
- Located at Plot 118, Block M, Visiga, Kibaha, Pwani. [official app_config]

### GCCP — Gulf Concrete and Cement Products Company Ltd (2010)
- Leading premix (ready-mix concrete) company in Dar es Salaam. [official gccp page]
- Own quarry at Lugoba with crushers. [official]
- Fully-automatic batching plants from Sany International (China); 2 aggregate plants of
  250 t/h each, ~30,000 m³/month aggregate; 4 stationary pumps + 2 boom pumps (32 m);
  20 truck mixers of 12 m³ each. [official]

### Gulf Aggregates (T) Ltd
- Started early 2012 as part of Gulf Concrete; first 3-stage 250 TPH crushing plant;
  second 4-stage 250 TPH plant 2015; company split end-2018 (GCCP = premix, Gulf
  Aggregates = aggregates); third plant 200 TPH added early 2019. Dump trucks load
  min. 20 m³. [official gulfagr app_config]

### AFICD — African Inland Container Depot
- ICD, CFS and empty-container-depot services; located Tazara, Pugu Road, Dar es Salaam;
  rail siding connecting to the port (~6 km); serves landlocked countries (Rwanda,
  Burundi, Uganda, DRC, Zambia, Malawi) via TAZARA rail up to Lubumbashi. [official]
- Yard 14,000 m², capacity 4,000 TEU, stacks 5-high; SAP-based operations; container
  repairs. [official]
- Shipping lines handled include COSCO, RAIS Shipping Line, Coral Shipping. [official]
- AFICD Mozambique Limitada: Manga Area, National Street No. 6, PO 2100, Beira. [official app_config]
- Zambia depot: Plot No. 39643/Mbwanamkubwa, Kabwe Road, opp. INDENI Petroleum, Ndola. [official app_config]

### ACFS — African Cargo Freight Station
- CFS arm launched off AFICD brand (AFICD ICD No. 022; CFS No. 013). Terminal: 13 acres /
  52,000 m², 5,000 TEU capacity (800 laden TEU ground slots = 4,000 TEU; 250 empty TEU
  ground slots = 1,000 TEU), 2 rail tracks, 2,100 m² warehouse, 80-ton weighbridge,
  32 reefer points (415 V 3-phase), 2 reach stackers, 2 forklifts (3 t & 7 t), 50 trucks
  and trailers; phase 2: 1 locomotive, 40 wagons, 1 empty-container handler. [official app_config TA.ACFS]

### MERM — Middle East Ready Mix LLC (Dubai)
- Ready-mix concrete producer in Dubai; production started mid-2005 (pre-dates the group;
  part of Lake Group per official site). [official merm page]

### SAFF — Star African Freight Forwarding LLC (Dubai)
- International freight: sea freight & marine services (FCL/LCL/RoRo/break-bulk/reefer),
  air freight & chartering (incl. Jet A1 fuelling), overland transport (FTL/LTL), customs
  clearance, 3PL, container trading, project cargo, vendor management. [official saff page]
- Dubai phone +971 4 2693040, fax +971 4 2693074. [official]
